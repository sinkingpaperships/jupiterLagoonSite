A Guide to Written Patterns

To understand written patterns, you will first need to know how to crochet. Don't overthink the process, all you need to start is yarn and a hook. If you do not crochet and would like to learn how, I highly recommend visiting Sarah Maker’s in-depth guide linked here to get started.

In the table below is a list of my free crochet patterns. In each table row, you will find the project’s name, recommended skill level, estimated completion time, and the link to a page with the product’s pattern.

Each pattern page lists an image of the completed project, the materials I used, shorthand references for crochet terms used within that pattern, and the full written pattern in terms of rounds.

Atmosphere

Following pattern instructions is important in crochet, but many people often overlook the importance of atmosphere. Having a nice environment to crochet in can greatly improve the creative process.

Some people may prefer to watch a show, sit by the window with a scented candle, listen to an audiobook or podcast, etc. I enjoy listening to music when I crochet. Here I have linked a few videos with playlists that I personally listen to and find helpful when crocheting. Maybe they can spark inspiration for you too.
